About Us

Our mission, history, and commitment to human rights education

Our Mission

We work toward creating a more caring and just society in which human rights and diversity are respected and valued. Through education, remembrance, and community engagement, we strive to ensure that the lessons of the Holocaust and other genocides are never forgotten.

Education

Providing comprehensive educational programs and resources

Remembrance

Honoring the memory of victims and survivors

Justice

Promoting human rights and social justice

Our History

Founded

1999

The Cape Town Holocaust & Genocide Centre was established to preserve the memory of the Holocaust and educate about the dangers of prejudice and discrimination.

Expansion

2000s

Our programs expanded to include education about other genocides and human rights violations, making us a comprehensive center for genocide education.

Recognition

2010s

Gained international recognition for our educational programs and became a leading voice in Holocaust and genocide education in Africa.

25 Years On

2024-2025

Celebrating 25 years of education, remembrance, and building a more just society through our various programs and initiatives.

Our Archives

Our extensive archives contain thousands of documents, photographs, testimonies, and artifacts that preserve the stories of survivors and document the history of the Holocaust and other genocides.
